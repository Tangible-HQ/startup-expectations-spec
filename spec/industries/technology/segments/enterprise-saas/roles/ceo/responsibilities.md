# CEO — enterprise SaaS

Read [shared expectations](../../../../../../shared/roles/ceo/responsibilities.md) first. This file adds what changes when your customers are businesses with procurement, security reviews, and multi-year contracts.

---

## At pre-seed

No additions to shared. Enterprise context isn't relevant until you have a customer or a clear enterprise ICP.

---

## At seed

**Enterprise sales involvement**
- Be present in late-stage deals — enterprise buyers want to meet the CEO before signing; this is expected, not a distraction
- Understand the procurement and security review process your customers run; you will be asked to fill out questionnaires and attend calls
- Customer relationships at this stage are personal relationships with the CEO; treat them that way

**Trust and compliance signals**
- Enterprise buyers will ask about security posture, data handling, and compliance before signing
- The CEO sets the tone for how seriously the company takes these — "we'll get to it later" is a visible answer
- Know which compliance certifications your target customers require and have a timeline for getting there

---

## At Series A

**Board and investor credibility in enterprise**
- Enterprise SaaS metrics (ARR, NRR, CAC, LTV, logo count) are what Series B investors will scrutinize; know them cold
- Customer references and case studies are a CEO responsibility — the relationships that make these possible are built personally

**Enterprise org design**
- Hire a Head of Sales or VP Sales with enterprise experience before the motion is fully documented — but only after you understand the sales cycle yourself
- Customer Success is not optional in enterprise; budget for it before churn becomes a problem
- Legal, security, and compliance functions grow at Series A because enterprise customers require them; plan for the overhead
