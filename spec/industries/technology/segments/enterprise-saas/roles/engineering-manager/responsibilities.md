# Engineering manager — enterprise SaaS

Read [shared expectations](../../../../../../shared/roles/engineering-manager/responsibilities.md) first. This file adds what changes when you sell subscription software to businesses — longer sales cycles, procurement, SLAs, and compliance.

---

## At pre-seed

No additions to shared. Enterprise context isn't relevant until you have a customer.

---

## At seed

**Customer-facing reliability**
- Enterprise buyers expect uptime commitments even in early contracts — know what you've promised and whether you can keep it
- Work with the founder or sales lead on urgent customer issues; a broken integration during a pilot is a churn event

**Compliance readiness**
- Security reviews and procurement questionnaires start arriving with your first enterprise prospects
- Begin tracking what access controls, data handling practices, and audit logs you have — this is the raw material for SOC 2 later
- Don't over-invest yet; document what exists, identify the biggest gaps, plan the roadmap

---

## At Series A

**Security and compliance program**
- Own the engineering side of SOC 2 or equivalent: access controls, change management, incident response documentation
- Coordinate with whoever owns legal and infosec on vendor reviews, pen tests, and audit timelines
- Build compliance work into the eng roadmap as a first-class item — not a sprint at the end of the quarter

**Enterprise sales support**
- Participate in late-stage deals when technical diligence or security reviews require engineering input
- Maintain release notes and changelog discipline; enterprise customers need predictable communication about changes
- Roadmap commitments made to anchor customers must be tracked and honored — flag early if they're at risk
