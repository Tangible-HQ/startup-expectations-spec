# Values and working norms

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Hiring & team

> Write down how you work before the team grows past the point where everyone knows intuitively — the norms you establish now become the culture you'll either keep or fight to change later.

---

## What you are aiming for

A short, honest document that describes how the team makes decisions, what behaviours are rewarded, how disagreement is handled, and what the default working style is. Not a list of aspirational adjectives. Specific enough that a new hire can read it and know whether they'll thrive here.

## What to focus on

- **Behaviours over adjectives** — "we are transparent" is a value; "we share project status updates weekly even when the news is bad" is a working norm; write norms, not slogans
- **Reward the behaviours you actually want** — if you say you value speed but only praise thoroughness, people notice; make sure what you reward in practice matches what you write down
- **Decide how decisions get made** — who has authority for what? does the CEO decide alone, or do founders consensus-vote? what's the escalation path? leaving this implicit creates conflict
- **Name the defaults** — async vs sync? bias toward shipping or toward quality? customer feedback vs founder intuition? these defaults shape hundreds of daily micro-decisions
- **Write it when you're 3–5 people** — at 10 people the norms are already baked in; writing them down is easier and more honest when the team is small enough that everyone is in the room

## Deprioritize for now

- Elaborate values-alignment surveys — too much process for a team of 5
- External culture consultants — founders should write this, not outsource it
- Annual culture reviews — revisit when the team doubles, not on a fixed calendar

## What to write down

**1. How decisions get made**
- Who has final authority for product, hiring, and financial decisions?
- What's the process when founders disagree?
- What can individual contributors decide without asking?

**2. Working style defaults**
- Async-first or sync-first?
- How much context should people share before asking for help?
- How much time is it OK to spend on polish vs shipping?

**3. How we handle disagreement**
- Is it OK to push back on a founder's decision in a team meeting?
- What's the norm for escalating a concern you've already raised?
- How do we decide when to revisit a decision that's already been made?

**4. What we reward and what we don't**
- What does a great week look like? Name specific behaviours.
- What are the things that would make someone a bad fit for this team?

**5. How we communicate**
- What belongs in a Slack message vs a doc vs a meeting?
- What's the expected response time for different channels?
- What's the norm for giving feedback — direct and immediate, or in 1:1s?

## What this document is not

It is not a list of core values for your website. It is not an HR policy. It is an internal working document that describes how this specific team operates in practice. Write it in first person. Write it honestly. Update it when you notice a gap between what's written and what actually happens.

## A note on culture carriers

The first 10 people you hire either reinforce or undermine the working norms. Someone who is technically excellent but doesn't share the working style — doesn't communicate async, escalates inappropriately, doesn't give feedback honestly — will over time reshape the norms in their direction. This is why culture fit is a legitimate hiring criterion, not an excuse for homogeneity.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — Implicit** | Norms exist but are unwritten; inconsistently applied; new hires have to infer them |
| **L1 — Written basics** | How decisions get made and working style defaults are written down |
| **L2 — Shared and applied** | Every team member has read and contributed to the norms; they're referenced in hiring and onboarding |
| **L3 — Living document** | Norms updated when gaps appear; behaviours rewarded in practice match what's written; new hires cite the document as useful |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Co-founder alignment** — working norms often expose co-founder disagreements; the conversation in [co-founder alignment](../co-founder-alignment/README.md) should precede writing this document
- **Interview process and bar** — culture fit should be evaluated explicitly in the interview loop, not as a vague vibe; [interview process and bar](../interview-process-and-bar/README.md) is where these norms become criteria
- **Onboarding and async comms** — this document is one of the first things a new hire should read; it should live in the onboarding doc from [onboarding and async comms](../onboarding-and-async-comms/README.md)
- **Operating rhythm** — the operating cadence in [operating rhythm](../operating-rhythm/README.md) expresses working norms in recurring practice; the two documents should be consistent
