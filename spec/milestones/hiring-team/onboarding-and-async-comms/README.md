# Onboarding and async communication

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Hiring & team

> A new hire's first week shapes their entire tenure — clear onboarding and async norms mean faster ramp, fewer misunderstandings, and culture transmitted deliberately rather than accidentally.

---

## What you are aiming for

A first-week experience for each new hire that gives them context, access, and a first win — without requiring a founder to be in meetings with them all day. And a communication system that works for a distributed or hybrid team without becoming an overhead machine.

## What to focus on

- **Document the first week before the hire starts** — write the day 1–5 plan in advance; improvised onboarding creates anxiety and wastes both parties' time
- **Context before tasks** — new hires need to understand the mission, the customers, and the current state of the product before they can contribute meaningfully; don't give them a ticket queue on day 1
- **Async by default, sync when necessary** — decisions that require back-and-forth need a meeting; status updates, feedback, and information sharing do not
- **One source of truth** — Notion, Confluence, or a shared drive; pick one and put everything there; information scattered across Slack, email, and Google Docs creates constant retrieval tax
- **Explicit over implicit** — things that seem obvious to the founding team are invisible to new hires; write down working norms, communication expectations, and decision-making conventions

## Deprioritize for now

- Formal 30/60/90 day performance plans — useful at 50 people, too heavy for 5
- Dedicated HR software for onboarding — a Notion checklist is sufficient
- Elaborate video onboarding courses — direct conversations and written docs outperform at this stage

## The minimum first-week plan

Write this document before the hire starts. It should cover:

**Day 1:**
- Accounts and access (company email, Slack, GitHub, product, staging env)
- First 1:1 with the founder — mission, context, current focus, working style
- Read: why we exist (problem), who our customers are, where we are now

**Day 2–3:**
- Understand the product: walk through the current product end-to-end
- Understand the customer: read 3–5 customer interview transcripts or onboarding notes
- Meet the rest of the team: 30-min intros with each team member

**Day 4–5:**
- First real task: something small and completable; not critical path, but real
- Debrief: what was unclear? what do you wish you'd known on day 1?
- Update the onboarding doc based on their feedback

## Async communication norms

Async-first means: default to written communication that doesn't require an immediate response. These norms help teams work across time zones or deep-focus blocks:

**What to put in writing (async):**
- Status updates on projects
- Decisions with context and rationale
- Feedback on documents, designs, or code
- Meeting agendas (posted 24 hours in advance)
- Meeting notes (posted within the hour)

**What warrants a sync:**
- Ambiguous or emotionally complex conversations
- Decisions that require real-time back-and-forth to resolve
- Unblocking a team member stuck on something
- Celebratory moments (wins, launches, milestones)

**Response time expectations:**
- Slack DM: within a few hours during working hours
- Slack channel: within the working day
- No expectation of reply outside working hours

## Tooling that works at seed stage

| Purpose | Tool |
|---------|------|
| Team communication | Slack |
| Documentation | Notion or Confluence |
| Code and technical docs | GitHub + inline docs |
| Video async | Loom |
| Project tracking | Linear or Jira (lightweight config) |

The problem is rarely the tool — it's whether everyone uses the same tool for the same purpose.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — Improvised** | No written onboarding; new hires figure things out; comms scattered across email and Slack |
| **L1 — First-week plan** | Written day 1–5 plan exists; accounts provisioned before start date |
| **L2 — Async norms** | Communication norms written down; single source of truth for docs; explicit response time expectations |
| **L3 — Self-improving** | Onboarding doc updated after each new hire; async norms revisited quarterly; new hires reach productivity in <2 weeks |
