# Interview process and bar

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Hiring & team

> A consistent, lightweight interview process protects you from bias and bad hires — without creating the overhead of a process built for a 500-person company.

---

## What you are aiming for

An interview loop small enough to move fast but structured enough that every candidate is evaluated against the same criteria. The goal is a process a founder can explain in one paragraph, run consistently without a recruiter, and improve based on whether it predicted good hires.

## What to focus on

- **Define the bar before you post the role** — write down the 3–5 things you're optimizing for; "smart and gets things done" is not a bar; "has shipped a production API used by external customers" is
- **Use work samples, not puzzles** — a take-home exercise or live problem on material the candidate might actually work on; algorithmic puzzles optimise for puzzle practice, not job performance
- **Structured scoring, not gut feel** — agree on scoring criteria before the interview; debrief immediately after; avoid letting the first opinion anchor everyone else
- **Move fast** — candidates at seed stage are often interviewing at multiple companies; a 3-week loop loses people; aim for offer to close in 5–10 business days
- **Founders close the offer** — the founder call after the loop is not a formality; candidates say yes because of the mission and the people, not the comp formula

## Deprioritize for now

- Panel interviews with 6 people — too slow and creates awkward group dynamics
- Automated ATS screening and HackerRank filters — you're hiring 1–2 people at a time; screen manually
- Competency frameworks with 20+ dimensions — more precision than your data can support at this stage

## A lightweight interview loop

**For engineering (4–6 hours total candidate time):**

1. **Intro call (30 min, founder)** — candidate questions, culture/mission, role details; filter for communication and basic fit
2. **Technical screen (45 min)** — live coding or system design on a real problem; evaluate thinking, not just the answer
3. **Work sample (2–3 hours, async)** — a take-home exercise based on a real problem the company has faced; pay candidates for their time ($100–200) as a signal of respect and to attract senior candidates
4. **Debrief loop (60 min, 2 people max)** — deep dive on the work sample; evaluate judgment and approach
5. **Founder close call (30 min)** — mission, vision, offer, negotiation

**For GTM/ops hires:**

Adapt the loop with a written work sample (e.g. draft an outbound sequence, model a CAC scenario) instead of technical screen.

## Scoring and debrief

Before each interview, write down what a strong answer looks like. After the interview, score independently before the group debrief. Use a simple rubric:

| Signal | Strong | Acceptable | Weak |
|--------|--------|------------|------|
| Problem clarity | Asks clarifying questions; frames the problem | Dives in without framing | Misunderstands the problem entirely |
| Judgment | Identifies trade-offs; knows what to deprioritize | Makes reasonable choices | No awareness of trade-offs |
| Communication | Clear, structured, adjusted to audience | Understandable | Hard to follow |

A "strong hire" requires strong scores on the signals that matter most for the role. Don't average across signals — a candidate who is weak on judgment is not saved by being strong on communication.

## Reference checks

Call at least 2 references for every hire. References provided by the candidate are still useful — they reveal how the candidate was perceived and what they're proud of. Ask:

- "What kind of work did [name] do best?"
- "What would you say is the one area they most need to develop?"
- "Would you hire them again, and for what kind of role?"

Back-channel references — reaching out to people who worked with the candidate but weren't provided as references — are the most informative for senior hires.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No process** | Each hire is a different experience; no scoring; decisions made by feel |
| **L1 — Consistent loop** | Every candidate goes through the same steps; at least one structured exercise |
| **L2 — Scored rubric** | Criteria defined before the interview; independent scoring; debrief structure |
| **L3 — Predictive** | You track whether the interview predicted performance; loop updated based on what worked |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Early team hires** — [early team hires](../early-team-hires/README.md) decides who to hire; this milestone is how you assess them consistently
- **Values and working norms** — the interview process should surface cultural fit as clearly as technical skill; [values and working norms](../values-and-working-norms/README.md) gives you the language for what you're looking for
- **Onboarding and async comms** — a good hire who lands in a poorly designed first week loses context and enthusiasm fast; the loop and the ramp are connected
