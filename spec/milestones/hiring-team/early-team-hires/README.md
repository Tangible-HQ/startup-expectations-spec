# Early team hires

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Hiring & team

> The first hires after the founding team set the culture, pace, and technical ceiling of the company — get the sequence and bar right before you scale headcount.

---

## What you are aiming for

A deliberate, sequenced hiring plan that matches team growth to company stage — not to fundraising FOMO. Each hire solves a specific bottleneck, can be onboarded without burning founder time, and raises the bar for the people who come after them.

## What to focus on

- **Sequence hires by bottleneck, not prestige** — hire the role that unblocks the next three months of progress; don't hire a VP of Marketing before you have product-market fit
- **Founders should hire the first 5–10 directly** — referral network, founder network, and warm intros; job boards at this stage attract a high volume of poor-fit candidates
- **Set a bar, then hire above it** — the first engineer you hire sets the implicit standard for the next five; if you lower the bar once, you lower it forever
- **First hires are function-starters, not function-fillers** — each early hire should be able to build the function from scratch, not just execute within it
- **Make the equity conversation clear and early** — candidates at seed stage are taking a risk; they need to understand the cap table, vesting, and the realistic upside

## Deprioritize for now

- Hiring for future org charts — don't hire a Head of X when what you need is an individual contributor doing X
- Agency recruiters for the first 10 hires — the cost is high and they can't sell the mission the way a founder can
- Elaborate compensation benchmarking tools — know the market range, but early hires are a negotiation, not a formula

## Typical first hire sequence for B2B SaaS

The right order depends on your founding team gaps, but a common pattern:

| Hire # | Role | What it unlocks |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Full-stack engineer | Product velocity when both founders are non-technical, or founders need to focus on sales |
| 2 | Account executive or SDR | Outbound pipeline when founders are product-focused |
| 3 | Full-stack engineer or designer | Product quality, design, or frontend velocity |
| 4 | Customer success or support | Retention and feedback loop at 10+ customers |
| 5 | Engineering lead | Team coordination when engineers > 3 |

Founding teams with a technical co-founder often don't need engineer hire #1 until post-seed.

## Red flags in early hiring

- **Hiring to fill a narrative** — e.g. hiring a VP of Sales to impress Series A investors before you've figured out what a repeatable sale looks like
- **Lowering the bar because you're desperate** — a weak hire on a 4-year vesting schedule is 4 years of drag
- **Skipping reference checks** — early hires have disproportionate influence; references reveal what a resume hides
- **Ignoring culture fit signals** — at 5 people, one person who doesn't share the working style makes every meeting harder

## What to tell candidates

At seed stage, candidates are evaluating mission, founders, and upside — not perks and brand. Be direct about:

- What the company does and who it's for
- Where you are: customers, revenue, runway
- What the role will actually look like in 6 months
- How equity works and what realistic outcomes look like
- What the founding team is like to work with

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — Ad hoc** | Hiring happens reactively; no plan; bar inconsistent across hires |
| **L1 — Plan exists** | Next 1–2 hires identified by role and priority; founders are sourcing |
| **L2 — Deliberate sequence** | 6-month hiring plan; sourcing from founder network; clear bar per role |
| **L3 — High-bar execution** | First 5+ hires are above-bar; references checked; onboarding documented; each hire raises team density |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Co-founder alignment** — hiring sequence should be aligned with the co-founder split from [co-founder alignment](../co-founder-alignment/README.md); don't hire to compensate for a co-founder problem
- **Interview process and bar** — once you know who to hire, [interview process and bar](../interview-process-and-bar/README.md) is how you assess them consistently
- **Onboarding and async comms** — each new hire needs a structured first week; [onboarding and async comms](../onboarding-and-async-comms/README.md) ensures the ramp is fast and the culture is transmitted
- **Runway model and buffer** — headcount is your largest cost driver; each hire should be modelled against runway before the offer goes out

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** You have a named list of the next 2–3 hires in priority order, an active sourcing plan for each, and a track record of hires who are above the bar you set at the start.
{% endhint %}
