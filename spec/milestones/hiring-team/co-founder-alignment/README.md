# Co-founder alignment

**Stage:** Pre-seed · **Category:** Team & hiring

> Have the hard conversations before you're under pressure — roles, departure terms, equity, and tie-breaking.

---

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Do this in the first 60 days.** The most common reason early startups fail isn't the market or the product — it's co-founder conflict. Most of it is preventable if you talk now, not after the company has value.
{% endhint %}

## What you are aiming for

A written agreement covering the scenarios that will stress your partnership, completed before you raise money or hire anyone. Not a legal formality — a forcing function for honest conversation while the stakes are still low.

## What to focus on

- **Decision authority** — who has final say on product direction, key hires, fundraising terms, and pivots; a 50/50 split with no tie-breaker is a deadlock waiting to happen
- **Departure terms** — what happens to equity if someone leaves in month 6 vs. month 18; whether it matters if they resign vs. are fired; agree before you need to use it
- **Vesting on all founder shares** — 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff; if you incorporated without it, fix it before any investor sees your cap table
- **IP assignment** — any code, designs, or inventions built before incorporation must be formally assigned to the company; missing IP assignment is the most common diligence flag at pre-seed
- **Salary and outside commitments** — what founders get paid now, what triggers a raise, and whether everyone is full-time; unspoken assumptions here cause resentment fast

## Deprioritize for now

- Elaborate shareholder agreements designed for later-stage companies — a clean founder agreement is enough at this stage
- Negotiating every edge case — cover the likely scenarios; exotic hypotheticals can wait

## The conversations to have now

Don't skip these because they feel premature. Every one of them becomes harder and more expensive after the company has value.

| Topic | What to decide |
|-------|----------------|
| Decision authority | Who has final say on product, key hires, fundraising, and pivots? |
| Departure terms | What happens to equity at month 6? Month 18? Fired vs. resigned? |
| Salary | What do founders earn now, and what triggers a raise? |
| Early exit | If you get a $3M acqui-hire offer in 18 months, what's the process? |
| Outside commitments | Is everyone full-time? If not, what's the trigger for that to change? |
| Tie-breaking | If you deadlock on a major decision, how is it resolved? |

## Key concepts

**Vesting** — equity earned over time, not granted upfront. Without it, a co-founder who leaves after six months walks away with a permanent undilutable stake. Standard: 4-year total, 1-year cliff, monthly vesting after.

**Good-leaver vs. bad-leaver** — when a founder departs, the company typically buys back unvested shares at cost. Good-leaver provisions (health, family) may allow more generous treatment than bad-leaver (fired for cause, competing against the company).

**IP assignment** — any work built before incorporation must be formally transferred to the company. If this is missing, investors will flag it in diligence and it can delay or kill a raise.

## What to formalize

A Founder Agreement should cover:

- Roles and decision ownership per founder
- Equity split and vesting schedule
- IP assignment to the company
- Departure terms and buyback rights
- Salary structure and how it changes
- What constitutes a "major decision" requiring joint agreement

Most startup lawyers offer flat-fee packages ($1,500–$3,000) for this. Do it once, do it properly.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — Nothing formalized** | No founder agreement; vesting may not be set up; key scenarios undiscussed |
| **L1 — Conversations started** | Topics raised verbally; vesting schedule in place; nothing else written down |
| **L2 — Agreement drafted** | Written co-founder agreement covering roles, vesting, IP, departure terms; all founders reviewed it |
| **L3 — Signed and current** | Agreement signed by all founders; legal review done; updated if circumstances have changed |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Founding roles and equity** — co-founder alignment assumes the equity split and role definitions are already decided; complete [founding roles and equity](../founding-roles-equity-decisions/README.md) first, then document the operational layer here
- **Early financing rounds** — investors will ask whether a co-founder agreement exists; not having one is a yellow flag that signals you're avoiding hard conversations
- **Cap table hygiene** — vesting schedules and buyback rights are part of cap table health; mismatches here surface at the seed round
- **Founder sustainability** — salary structure and runway for founders is part of this conversation; the two milestones overlap on compensation

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** Every founder has read and signed a written agreement covering roles, vesting, IP assignment, departure terms, salary, and tie-breaking. The test: if one founder left tomorrow, would you know exactly what happens to their equity and who makes decisions next?
{% endhint %}
