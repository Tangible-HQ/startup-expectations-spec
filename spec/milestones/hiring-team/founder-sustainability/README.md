# Founder sustainability

**Stage:** Pre-seed · **Category:** Team & hiring

> Set a salary floor and a sustainable pace so founders don't make desperate decisions.

---

## What you are aiming for

A setup where every founder can operate for 18+ months without personal financial stress forcing a bad business decision. Not comfort — sustainability. The goal is to remove the pressure that leads founders to close the wrong customer, accept bad terms, or make premature hires.

## What to focus on

- **Set a salary floor, not zero** — founders working for nothing will eventually face a personal runway crisis; agree on a minimum salary before it becomes urgent
- **Separate personal runway from company runway** — know how long you personally can operate at your current salary before you're in trouble; this is a different number from company runway
- **Agree on salary triggers** — decide now what event (funding, first revenue, specific milestone) triggers a raise; don't leave it open-ended
- **Name the unsustainable patterns early** — 80-hour weeks for months degrade decision quality; founders who burn out make worse decisions in months 8–12 than rested founders do in month 6
- **Co-founder parity** — founders with very different financial situations create silent resentment; surface the disparity and address it

## Deprioritize for now

- Market-rate salaries — you're trading salary for equity; below-market is expected and correct at pre-seed
- Elaborate wellness programmes — the basics (sleep, exercise, not working every weekend) are what matter
- Formal HR policies — those come at seed; right now you need a working agreement between founders

## What "sustainable" looks like at each stage

**Pre-seed:** A salary that covers basic living expenses without drawing down personal savings. The number varies by city and personal situation — the point is that it exists and is agreed. Typical range: $60k–$120k/year depending on location and obligations.

**Seed (post-raise):** Raise founder salaries to a level that removes financial distraction. Seed-stage norms vary widely — $100k–$175k is common. The bar is: can each founder focus entirely on the company without worrying about rent?

**Series A:** Salaries approach market rate for the role. The equity premium is still real, but financial stress should not be a factor in founder decision-making at this point.

## The decisions to make now

| Decision | What to agree |
|----------|---------------|
| Current salary | What each founder draws starting now |
| Salary trigger | What milestone or event triggers a raise |
| Leave policy | How time off works — even if informal |
| Working hours | What "normal" looks like; what "crunch" looks like and how long it's sustainable |
| Personal runway floor | The minimum personal savings each founder must maintain; a tripwire for raising salary or taking on less risk |

## Common gotchas

- **"We'll figure it out later"** — salary conversations feel awkward when the company is exciting; they feel worse when someone is broke and resentful
- **Founder pays themselves nothing** — this is often celebrated as commitment; it's actually a countdown to a crisis
- **Unequal situations left unaddressed** — a founder with dependents and a mortgage is in a different position than a founder with no obligations; pretending otherwise doesn't make it true
- **No salary trigger defined** — "we'll raise salaries when we can afford it" leads to founders raising their own salary at different times and without a shared frame
- **Ignoring pace until it breaks** — by the time burnout is obvious, months of productivity have already been lost

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No agreement** | Founders working for free or inconsistently; no salary discussion has happened |
| **L1 — Floor set** | Minimum salary agreed and being paid; no trigger for raises defined |
| **L2 — Fully agreed** | Salary, trigger for raises, and working norms agreed between all founders |
| **L3 — Sustainable rhythm** | Founders operating at agreed pace; salary reviewed at funding events; no one in personal financial distress |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Co-founder alignment** — salary structure is part of the co-founder agreement; do [co-founder alignment](../co-founder-alignment/README.md) first so compensation decisions are made jointly, not unilaterally
- **Company financial backbone** — founder salaries run through payroll; [company financial backbone](../../operations-back-office/company-financial-backbone/README.md) must be in place before payroll can be set up
- **Early financing rounds** — use-of-funds conversations with investors will include founder salaries; know your number before the pitch
