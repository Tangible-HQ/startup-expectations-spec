# Shipping your MVP

**Stage:** Pre-seed → Seed · **Category:** Build & ship

> Turn a scoped MVP into something in real users' hands at an explicit quality level — with learning captured and another ship cycle still possible.

---

## What you are aiming for

A working product at a declared level of quality that a real user can interact with, so you can observe whether your core hypothesis is correct. This milestone is about **execution and learning**, not re-litigating scope — scope belongs in [Defining MVP scope](../defining-mvp-scope/README.md).

## What to focus on

- **One sharp hypothesis per ship** — know what you are trying to learn before you ship, not after
- **Level-appropriate quality** — match your quality bar to who will use it (see levels below)
- **Document the gaps** — honest disclosure of known gaps protects trust and focuses the next iteration
- **Feed learning forward** — the output of a ship is a decision: continue, narrow, or stop

## Deprioritize for now

Feature breadth, long-term scale proofs, or full GTM readiness until this level's outcomes are met.

## Quality levels

Levels are not seniority — they are **release rigor**: who may use the product and what failure modes you accept.

| Level | Who uses it | What quality means |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| **L0 — Internal / dogfood** | Team only | Prove build and integration; not market proof |
| **L1 — Private cohort** | Invited users | Learning-first; manual ops acceptable; cohort-safe |
| **L2 — Limited beta** | Real users under caps | Daily use expected; reliability and support start to matter |
| **L3 — Public MVP** | Anyone | Abuse, privacy, and ops must match public exposure |

{% hint style="info" %}
Advance levels only when the previous level's learning supports it. Jumping from L0 to L3 to "move fast" skips the learning — it does not compress it.
{% endhint %}

## How this connects to other goals

- **Customers** — a shipped MVP at L1+ produces signals for [first paying users](../../../revenue-gtm/first-paying-users-design-partners/README.md) and [feedback loop](../../../revenue-gtm/feedback-loop-metrics/README.md). No ship level guarantees revenue; it enables evidence.
- **Funding** — investors want hypothesis-observable + learning-captured tied to a narrative. L0 rarely counts as traction; L1–L2 often do at early stage.
- **Trust** — L3 almost always requires [security and privacy posture](../../../trust-compliance/security-and-privacy-posture/README.md) and [incident readiness](../../../trust-compliance/incident-readiness/README.md) to be in place.

## Team impact

Engineering leadership: prioritize release criteria, risk disclosure, and learning loops over portfolio polish. Align with product and customers milestones so the same metrics are not reinterpreted after the fact.
