# Runway dashboard and burn reviews

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Operations

> Track spend against milestones on a fixed cadence so you are never surprised by a cash-out date.

---

## What you are aiming for

A single source of truth for cash position, burn rate, and runway — reviewed on a fixed cadence by the founding team and shared with investors on a regular schedule. Not a spreadsheet that gets updated when someone remembers. A practice that runs automatically every week.

## What to focus on

- **One number everyone agrees on** — if the CEO and CFO quote different burn rates, you have a data problem; pick one source and stick to it
- **Actuals vs forecast, not just actuals** — tracking what you spent is useful; tracking how that compares to what you planned to spend is what enables decisions
- **Weekly for founders, monthly for investors** — internal burn reviews weekly; investor-facing updates monthly or at board meetings; different cadences for different audiences
- **Runway in months, not dollars** — "we have $800k left" is less useful than "we have 11 months of runway at current burn"; months are what create urgency or comfort

## Deprioritize for now

- An elaborate BI platform with custom dashboards — a well-maintained spreadsheet or a simple finance tool is enough at seed
- Daily reporting — weekly is sufficient; daily is noise
- Automated variance analysis — manual review is fine until you have a finance hire

## What to review each week

**5-minute weekly check (founders only):**
- Current cash balance (check the bank account)
- Estimated burn this month vs last month
- Any unexpected expenses or delayed revenues?
- Are we on the runway path we expected?

**30-minute monthly review (founders + any finance support):**
- Actual P&L vs budget: where did we over- or underspend?
- Updated runway estimate under base and worst case
- Any changes to hiring plan that affect burn?
- What's the earliest date we should start a fundraising process if needed?

**Investor update (monthly or at board meetings):**
- Cash balance and months of runway
- Burn rate: actual vs plan
- Revenue and pipeline if applicable
- Any changes to the plan

## The dashboard (minimum viable)

At seed, a dashboard doesn't need to be sophisticated. It needs to be current and agreed upon.

| Metric | Source | Updated |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Cash balance | Bank account | Weekly |
| Monthly burn | Bookkeeping software | Monthly close |
| Months of runway | Cash ÷ burn | Monthly |
| Burn vs budget | Bookkeeping vs financial model | Monthly |
| Next fundraise trigger date | Runway floor calculation | Monthly |

**Tools:** QuickBooks or Xero for actuals, a spreadsheet for the model, Notion or Google Docs for the investor update. Add a dedicated tool (Mosaic, Finmark, Runway) when you hire a finance person.

## The runway trigger rule

Define in advance: when you hit X months of runway, you start a fundraising process immediately.

Common trigger: **9 months of runway** — enough time to run a 3–4 month fundraising process and close before you run out.

If you hit the trigger and decide not to raise, you must cut burn enough to extend runway back above the trigger. This is the decision: raise or cut. Doing neither is not an option.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No cadence** | Burn reviewed irregularly; no dashboard; cash-out date unknown |
| **L1 — Monthly review** | Monthly burn review happening; founders know runway in months |
| **L2 — Actuals vs forecast** | Budget exists; monthly actuals compared to budget; variance explained |
| **L3 — Investor-visible** | Dashboard shared with investors monthly; trigger rule defined; weekly founder check-in running |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Runway model and buffer** — this milestone is the operational cadence built on top of the model from [runway model and buffer](../../fundraising-runway/runway-model-and-buffer/README.md); the model is the structure, the review cadence is the practice
- **Board and investor reporting** — monthly burn reviews feed directly into investor updates; align the format with [board and investor reporting](../../fundraising-runway/board-investor-reporting-rhythm/README.md)
- **Next-round unlock milestones** — the dashboard should show progress toward your unlock milestones alongside burn; fundraising readiness and cash position should be reviewed together
