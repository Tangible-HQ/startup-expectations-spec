# Early financing rounds

**Stage:** Pre-seed → Seed · **Category:** Funding & runway

> Raise the minimum amount that gets you to your next fundable milestone — not more, not less.

---

## What you are aiming for

A closed round — with money in the bank — that gives you 12–18 months of runway to reach a specific, named milestone that justifies the next raise at better terms. Not the highest valuation. Not the most capital. The right amount from the right people.

## What to focus on

- **Name the milestone first** — know the one proof point this round is designed to reach before deciding how much to raise; "we'll use it to grow" is not a milestone
- **Size the round from the milestone** — work backwards: cost per month × months to milestone × 1.25 buffer = raise amount
- **Use of funds must be specific** — investors will ask; a vague answer signals you don't know how to run the company
- **Investor quality over valuation** — a lower cap from someone who opens 10 doors beats a higher cap from someone who adds nothing
- **Don't spend before the wire clears** — verbal commitments and signed SAFEs are not money

## Deprioritize for now

- Optimising valuation at the expense of closing speed or investor quality
- Complex instrument structures — a standard capped SAFE is sufficient for most pre-seed rounds
- Raising more than you need — unnecessary dilution is real and compounds

## How early financing is structured

Most pre-seed and seed rounds use simple instruments rather than priced equity rounds.

### SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)

The most common instrument for pre-seed. A SAFE is not equity — it's a promise to convert into equity at a future priced round.

**Key terms:**
- **Valuation cap** — the maximum valuation at which the SAFE converts; protects the investor if you raise at a high valuation
- **Discount** — converts at a percentage discount to the next round price (e.g. 20% discount means they pay $0.80 for every $1.00 of the next round)
- **MFN** — gives the investor the right to adopt better terms if you issue future SAFEs with better terms

### Convertible note

Similar to a SAFE but structured as debt — accrues interest, has a maturity date. Less common for new rounds but some investors still prefer it. Key difference: it is legally debt; a noteholder can theoretically demand repayment if the company doesn't raise before maturity.

### Priced round

Full equity financing with a negotiated valuation and term sheet. Standard for Seed rounds of $2M+ and universal at Series A. Key terms: pre-money valuation, pro-rata rights, board seats.

## Stage-to-instrument map

| Stage | Typical instrument | Typical raise size | What it needs to unlock |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Pre-seed | SAFE (capped) | $250k–$1.5M | Proof of concept, ICP identified, team formed |
| Seed | SAFE or priced round | $1M–$4M | First paying users or clear validation signal |
| Seed extension | SAFE or bridge note | $500k–$2M | Didn't hit Series A metrics; buys time |
| Series A | Priced round | $5M–$20M | Repeatable revenue motion, team of 8–15 |

## How to size the round

Work backwards from the milestone:
1. What is the one milestone that makes the next raise at better terms possible?
2. What does it cost per month to operate at the team size needed to reach it?
3. How many months will it realistically take?
4. Multiply monthly burn × months × 1.25–1.3 buffer = target raise amount

## Who to raise from

| Source | What they offer | Watch out for |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| Angels | Fast decisions, domain expertise | No institutional support |
| Pre-seed funds | Portfolio network, some infrastructure | Pro-rata expectations that complicate your seed |
| Accelerators (YC, Techstars) | Network, credibility, demo day | Equity cost; time commitment of the programme |
| Friends and family | Fastest and least conditional | Can damage relationships; no value-add |
| Strategic investors | Market validation, customer relationships | Slow, complex terms, future investor concerns |

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No strategy** | No instrument chosen; no milestone defined; no investor conversations |
| **L1 — In process** | Round size and instrument decided; investor conversations started; milestone named |
| **L2 — Commitments received** | Signed SAFEs or term sheet in hand; round not yet closed |
| **L3 — Round closed** | Funds wired, cap table updated, use-of-funds plan documented |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Runway model and buffer** — your round size should be derived from your runway model; raise before doing [runway model](../runway-model-and-buffer/README.md) and you're guessing the ask
- **ICP and wedge** — investors fund milestones, not ideas; raising before [ICP and wedge](../../revenue-gtm/icp-and-wedge/README.md) is validated means selling a thesis, not evidence
- **Grants and accelerators** — non-dilutive capital can reduce the size of the round you need; evaluate [grants and accelerators](../grants-accelerators-strategic/README.md) before finalising round size
- **Cap table hygiene** — each SAFE adds complexity; understand the conversion implications before stacking instruments

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Common traps**

- **Stacking SAFEs at different caps** — creates a conversion waterfall that surprises founders at Series A
- **Raising too early** — raising before you have a validated problem means selling a thesis at worse terms
- **Raising too little** — running out three months before your milestone is the worst time to raise again
- **Spending before the wire clears** — signed SAFEs are not money; do not hire until funds are in the bank
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** Funds are wired, the cap table is updated, and you can state in one sentence what milestone this round is designed to prove and in how many months.
{% endhint %}
