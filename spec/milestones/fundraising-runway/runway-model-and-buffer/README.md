# Runway model and buffer

**Stage:** Pre-seed · **Category:** Funding & runway

> Know exactly how long you can operate, what it costs to get to the next milestone, and the minimum cash floor you will not go below.

---

## What you are aiming for

A live model — updated at least monthly — that tells you burn rate, months of runway under realistic scenarios, and the minimum cash buffer below which you must act. Not a spreadsheet you built once and forgot. A decision-making tool you actually use.

## What to focus on

- **Know your monthly burn** — total cash out each month including payroll, tools, hosting, and everything else; a number you don't know will surprise you
- **Model hiring sensitivity** — each new hire changes your runway materially; run the numbers before committing, not after
- **Set a hard floor** — define the minimum cash balance you will not go below without taking action; 3–4 months of burn is a common floor
- **Run scenarios, not just a base case** — best, base, and worst; the base case is usually optimistic; the worst case is what you need to be ready for
- **Review on a cadence** — monthly minimum; weekly during a raise or when burn is elevated

## Deprioritize for now

- Complex models with dozens of assumptions — simple and current beats elaborate and stale
- Revenue projections at pre-seed — model costs accurately; revenue is speculation until you have paying customers
- Multi-year forecasts — 18 months is the planning horizon that matters

## How to build the model

**Step 1 — Monthly burn:**
Fixed costs (payroll, rent, subscriptions) + variable costs (hosting, contractors, travel) = monthly burn rate.

**Step 2 — Runway:**
Current cash ÷ monthly burn = months of runway. Below 9 months means you should be fundraising now.

**Step 3 — Hiring sensitivity:**
For each planned hire: salary + benefits + equipment + recruiting cost. Recalculate runway with and without. The question: does this hire get you to the next milestone faster than the runway it costs?

**Step 4 — Buffer floor:**
Pick a minimum cash balance you will not go below without action. When you hit it, two options: raise (if close enough to a milestone) or cut burn.

**Step 5 — Three scenarios:**

| Scenario | Assumptions | Use |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| Best case | Hiring as planned, revenue earlier than expected | Sanity check |
| Base case | Hiring as planned, revenue at expected timeline | Day-to-day planning |
| Worst case | Delayed hiring, revenue later than expected | Risk management and floor-setting |

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No visibility** | Spending from a bank account with no model; founders cannot state runway in months |
| **L1 — Basic spreadsheet** | Monthly burn calculated; rough runway estimated; reviewed at least monthly |
| **L2 — Scenario model** | Best/base/worst scenarios documented; hiring sensitivity modelled; minimum buffer floor defined |
| **L3 — Live dashboard** | Connected to actuals (bank, payroll, card feeds); scenarios updated monthly; board or investors see the same view as founders |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Company financial backbone** — you can't model burn accurately without clean books; set up [company financial backbone](../../operations-back-office/company-financial-backbone/README.md) first
- **Early financing rounds** — your round size should come directly from this model; raise without a runway model and you're guessing the ask
- **Founder sustainability** — founder salaries are a line item in the model; do [founder sustainability](../../hiring-team/founder-sustainability/README.md) and runway modelling together
- **Runway dashboard and burn reviews** — at seed, this model graduates into a more formal dashboard reviewed with investors; [runway dashboard](../../operations-back-office/runway-dashboard-burn-reviews/README.md) is the next stage of this practice

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** You can state monthly burn, months of runway in each scenario, and your cash floor — and every co-founder agrees on the number that triggers a fundraising process or a cut.
{% endhint %}
