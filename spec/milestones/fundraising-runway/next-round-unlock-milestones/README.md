# Milestones that unlock the next round

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Funding & runway

> Know the exact proof points Series A investors expect to see — and build toward them from day one of the seed, not month 14.

---

## What you are aiming for

A small set of measurable targets — defined before you spend your seed capital — that, when hit, make a Series A conversation inevitable rather than speculative. Not vanity metrics. Not "strong growth." Specific numbers tied to a specific narrative.

## What to focus on

- **Define the targets before you spend the money** — "we'll figure out the metrics as we go" means Series A investors will define them for you, at a disadvantage
- **Pick 2–4 metrics maximum** — more than four unlock milestones means you haven't prioritised; investors will pick the weakest one and ask why you missed it
- **Connect metrics to narrative** — a number without a story is just a number; the unlock milestone should complete the sentence "we proved X, which means Y is now possible at scale"
- **Track them weekly** — if you're not on track at month 6, you have time to adjust; if you notice at month 14, you don't
- **Know what Series A investors in your space care about** — talk to 3 Series A investors before spending your seed round; ask them directly what would make you fundable

## What Series A investors typically look for

These are illustrative benchmarks, not rules — they shift by market, year, and investor. Use them as a starting frame.

| Signal | Typical threshold | What it proves |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| ARR | $1M–$3M | Revenue is real and recurring |
| MoM growth rate | 10–15% | The business is growing meaningfully |
| Net Revenue Retention | >100% | Customers expand; churn is low |
| Paying customer count | 10–50 (B2B) | Multiple customers, not one big bet |
| Gross margin | >60% (SaaS) | The business model is sound |
| CAC payback period | <18 months | Acquisition efficiency is reasonable |

**The most important signal beyond the numbers:** a repeatable customer acquisition motion that a non-founder can execute. Revenue with no reproducible path to more revenue does not unlock a Series A.

## How to set your unlock milestones

**Step 1 — Talk to 3 Series A investors** in your category. Ask: "What would you need to see from a company like ours to lead a Series A?" Record exactly what they say.

**Step 2 — Map your milestones to their answers.** If three investors independently mention NRR and payback period, those are your milestones. If only one mentions something, weight it less.

**Step 3 — Work backwards from the milestone to today.** If you need $1.5M ARR in 18 months and are at $0 today, what do you need to close in months 1, 6, 12? If the path doesn't add up, either the milestone is wrong or the plan is.

**Step 4 — Write it down and share it.** Your seed investors should know your unlock milestones. They will hold you to them and help you get there.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No targets** | No defined milestones; building toward a vague "Series A readiness" |
| **L1 — Targets identified** | 2–4 specific metrics named; not yet tracked or on a path |
| **L2 — On track** | Milestones defined, tracked weekly, current trajectory consistent with hitting them |
| **L3 — Targets hit** | Unlock milestones reached; Series A fundraising process started or investor conversations active |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Raising a seed round** — your seed round is designed to get you to these milestones; size the raise in [early financing rounds](../early-financing-rounds/README.md) based on what it costs to hit them
- **Runway model and buffer** — unlock milestones are the destination in your runway model; the model tells you whether you can afford to get there in time
- **Unit economics experiments** — CAC payback and gross margin are two of the most common unlock milestones; [unit economics experiments](../unit-economics-experiments/README.md) are how you measure them
- **Investor pipeline and diligence** — when you hit your milestones, the fundraising process starts; have the pipeline and data room ready before you hit them, not after

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** You can name 2–4 specific metrics, their target values, your current values, and the date by which you expect to hit them — and your seed investors know and agree with the same list.
{% endhint %}
