# Investor pipeline and diligence

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Funding & runway

> Run a tight fundraising process — tracked pipeline, a clear ask, and a data room ready before diligence starts.

---

## What you are aiming for

A fundraising process that runs like a sales process: a defined list of target investors, a consistent pitch, tracked stages, and a data room that can survive diligence without scrambling. The goal is to compress the time from first meeting to close — slow processes lose momentum and let investors over-think.

## What to focus on

- **Create artificial scarcity** — running a process with multiple investors simultaneously creates urgency; drip-feeding one investor at a time gives them no reason to move fast
- **Track every conversation** — use a simple spreadsheet or CRM; know the stage of every investor; follow up on a defined cadence
- **Prepare the data room before you need it** — investors who like the pitch will ask for diligence materials immediately; having them ready signals operational maturity
- **Know your metrics cold** — you will be asked about every number in your deck; an uncertain answer to "what's your NRR?" loses trust fast
- **Protect product time** — a raise that consumes 100% of founder attention kills the metrics you're fundraising on; protect at least 40–50% of time for the business

## Deprioritize for now

- Perfect slide design — clarity of narrative matters more than visual polish
- Chasing every inbound intro regardless of fit — 20 targeted investors beat 100 random ones
- Negotiating every term before you have a lead — get a lead first, negotiate terms second

## The fundraising process

**Phase 1 — Preparation (2–4 weeks before outreach)**
- Finalise the deck: problem, solution, traction, team, market, ask
- Build the target investor list: 20–40 names, prioritised by fit and relationship warmth
- Prepare the data room (see below)
- Know your metrics: ARR, MoM growth, NRR, CAC, LTV, burn, runway

**Phase 2 — Outreach and first meetings (weeks 1–3)**
- Get warm intros where possible; cold outreach works but warm intros convert 3–5x better
- Run first meetings in a tight window — 2–3 weeks; this creates natural parallelism
- First meeting goal: get to a second meeting; don't try to close in meeting one

**Phase 3 — Diligence and close (weeks 3–8)**
- Share data room proactively after second meetings
- Maintain parallel processes; don't pause for one investor
- Get a term sheet, negotiate key terms, then move other investors to close or drop

## The data room

A seed-stage data room doesn't need to be comprehensive — it needs to answer the questions investors will ask.

**Must-have documents:**
- Deck (same as pitch, or a longer appendix version)
- Financial model: P&L, burn rate, 18-month forecast
- Cap table: current ownership, option pool, outstanding SAFEs
- Key metrics dashboard: ARR, MRR, growth, NRR, CAC (whatever is relevant)
- Customer list: anonymised or named depending on permission

**Nice to have:**
- Product demo video or live demo link
- Reference customer contacts (2–3 customers willing to take calls)
- Legal: incorporation documents, IP assignment agreements
- Team bios or LinkedIn profiles

**Tools:** Notion, Google Drive, or a dedicated data room tool (Docsend, Briefcase) — the tool matters less than having the documents ready.

## Handling diligence questions

Diligence is not an exam — it's a trust-building exercise. Investors are confirming the story you told in the pitch.

- Answer questions completely and quickly; slow or incomplete answers signal something is wrong
- Be honest about weak spots; investors will find them; getting ahead of them builds credibility
- Reference customers are your most powerful diligence asset; brief them before they get called

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No process** | No deck, no investor list, no tracking; conversations happening ad hoc |
| **L1 — Deck and list** | Pitch deck exists; target investor list built; no tracking of conversations |
| **L2 — Active process** | Pipeline tracked; first meetings running; clear ask defined |
| **L3 — Diligence-ready** | Data room complete; metrics documented; reference customers briefed; term sheet in hand or close |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Next-round unlock milestones** — you should only start this process when you've hit or are very close to your [unlock milestones](../next-round-unlock-milestones/README.md); fundraising on weak metrics is harder and distracts from fixing them
- **Runway model and buffer** — know your runway before entering a process; running out of money mid-raise is the worst negotiating position
- **Cap table hygiene** — investors will scrutinise your cap table in diligence; [cap table hygiene](../../operations-back-office/cap-table-hygiene/README.md) must be clean before sharing it

**Done when:** You have a term sheet in hand, a data room that answered all diligence questions, and a clear path to close — with funds expected in the bank within 30 days.
{% endhint %}
