# Grants, accelerators, and strategic capital

**Stage:** Pre-seed · **Category:** Funding & runway

> Non-dilutive and program-based funding can extend runway or add network value — but each has a real cost in time and equity that is worth calculating before applying.

---

## What you are aiming for

A clear decision on which programmes or non-dilutive sources fit your stage and goals — pursued selectively, not reflexively. The default is not to apply to everything; it's to identify the one or two options that materially change your trajectory.

## What to focus on

- **Calculate the time cost** — accelerator programmes typically require 3 months of intensive participation; that's 3 months not spent on customers and product; only worth it if the network and signal outweigh the opportunity cost
- **Understand the equity cost** — YC takes 7%; Techstars takes 6%; strategic investors may want board seats or information rights; model the dilution before signing
- **Non-dilutive first** — grants, cloud credits, and R&D tax credits cost nothing in equity; always evaluate these before taking dilutive capital
- **Strategic capital requires extra scrutiny** — a corporate investor who owns 10% of your pre-seed can complicate future VC rounds; get explicit clearance from your lead investor before closing

## Deprioritize for now

- Applying to accelerators for credibility alone — the value is in the network and the signal, not the badge
- Pursuing grants that take more time to apply for than the grant is worth
- Taking strategic capital without understanding the downstream cap table implications

## Accelerators worth knowing

| Programme | Equity | What you get | Best for |
|-----------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Y Combinator | 7% | $500k, network, demo day, alumni | Almost any stage; strongest signal in the market |
| Techstars | 6% + $20k | Mentorship, corporate partnerships, demo day | Industry-specific tracks (fintech, retail, etc.) |
| Antler | Varies | Pre-idea support, co-founder matching, seed capital | Very early founders who haven't started yet |
| On Deck | Minimal | Community, peer cohort, introductions | Founders who already have a company but want network |

**On YC specifically:** the network, signal, and demo day audience are worth the dilution for most early-stage companies. Apply if you're pre-product and pre-revenue. The batch experience is valuable; the alumni network lasts forever.

## Non-dilutive sources worth knowing

| Source | Amount | Time cost | Best for |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| SBIR/STTR (US) | $150k–$2M | High (lengthy application) | Deep tech, life sciences, defence-adjacent |
| NSF I-Corps | $50k | Medium | Academic spinouts validating commercial potential |
| Cloud credits (AWS, GCP, Azure) | $25k–$100k | Low | Any startup with meaningful compute costs |
| R&D tax credits | 10–25% of R&D spend | Low (accountant handles) | Any company spending on engineering |
| State/regional grants | Varies | Medium | Location-dependent; worth checking local programmes |

**Cloud credits in particular** are almost always worth pursuing — AWS Activate, Google for Startups, and Microsoft for Startups all offer meaningful credits with minimal application friction.

## Strategic capital

Strategic investors (corporates, industry incumbents) can offer market validation and customer relationships alongside capital. The risks:

- **Future VC concerns** — institutional investors may ask hard questions about why a competitor or potential acquirer owns part of the company
- **Slow process** — corporate investment processes are slower than angels or funds; can delay your close
- **Information rights** — strategic investors often negotiate for board observer seats or information rights; understand what you're sharing and with whom
- **Acqui-hire risk perception** — a strategic investor at pre-seed can make the company look like it's positioning for acquisition rather than independent growth

**Rule of thumb:** strategic capital makes sense when the partner brings a specific customer relationship or distribution advantage that meaningfully accelerates the business. Capital alone is not a sufficient reason.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — Not evaluated** | No assessment of what programmes or grants apply to this company |
| **L1 — Options identified** — | Relevant accelerators, grants, and credits researched; decision made on which to pursue |
| **L2 — In process** | Applications submitted or programme accepted; cloud credits activated |
| **L3 — Closed** | Programme completed or grant received; cap table impact of any equity taken is modelled |

## How this connects to other goals

- **Early financing rounds** — grants and cloud credits reduce how much you need to raise from investors; evaluate non-dilutive options before finalising your round size in [early financing rounds](../early-financing-rounds/README.md)
- **Runway model and buffer** — non-dilutive capital extends runway without dilution; model it in your [runway model](../runway-model-and-buffer/README.md) as a separate line
- **ICP and wedge** — accelerator applications require a clear ICP and problem statement; completing [ICP and wedge](../../revenue-gtm/icp-and-wedge/README.md) first makes the application stronger

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** You have evaluated the relevant options for your stage and sector, made an explicit decision on which to pursue (including a decision not to pursue any), and can explain why in terms of time cost, equity cost, and strategic value.
{% endhint %}

- [SBIR.gov](https://www.sbir.gov) — official source for US government SBIR and STTR grant programmes; searchable by agency and technology area
- [AWS Activate](https://aws.amazon.com/activate), [Google for Startups](https://startup.google.com), [Microsoft for Startups](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/startups) — apply to all three; credits stack and the applications are low effort
