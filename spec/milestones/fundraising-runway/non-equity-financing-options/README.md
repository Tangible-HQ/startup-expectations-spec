# Non-equity financing options

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Funding & runway

> Venture debt, revenue-based financing, and cloud credits can extend runway without dilution — but each comes with covenants, cost of capital, and timing constraints that founders often underestimate.

---

## What you are aiming for

An informed understanding of the non-equity financing tools available at seed stage — when they make sense, what they cost, and when to avoid them. For some companies, non-equity financing materially extends runway or bridges to the next equity round. For others, the covenants and constraints make them the wrong choice. Know which situation you're in before signing.

## What to focus on

- **Non-equity doesn't mean free** — venture debt has interest rates and covenants; revenue-based financing has revenue share obligations; cloud credits come with vendor lock-in risk; understand the true cost before taking the capital
- **Timing matters more than amount** — non-equity financing taken after a strong equity round from a position of strength is a leverage tool; taken from desperation to avoid a down round, it's usually a mistake
- **Covenants can constrain future decisions** — venture debt often includes material adverse change (MAC) covenants, minimum cash requirements, and lender rights that limit your operating flexibility; read them before signing
- **Cloud credits are often the best deal** — AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure credits through startup programs are essentially free money; apply early and renew every year
- **Don't mistake bridge financing for business model validation** — non-equity capital extends runway; it doesn't solve the underlying problems that prevent equity fundraising

## Deprioritize for now

- Revenue-based financing before you have predictable revenue — it's designed for businesses with recurring revenue and predictable growth; before product-market fit it's mispriced risk
- Venture debt from lenders unfamiliar with your sector — covenant terms vary widely; use lenders with a track record in venture-backed startups

## Types of non-equity financing

**Venture debt**
Loans from venture-focused lenders (Silicon Valley Bank, Hercules Capital, Western Technology Investment, etc.) typically taken alongside or after an equity round. Structure:
- Loan amount: typically 20–30% of the equity round raised
- Term: 24–36 months with an interest-only period followed by amortisation
- Interest: variable, typically Prime + 2–5%
- Warrants: lenders typically take warrants for 1–2% of the loan value as additional compensation
- Covenants: minimum cash balance, MAC clause, often a "no material adverse change" provision

**Best for:** extending runway between rounds without dilution; funding capex or working capital for companies with predictable revenue

**Revenue-based financing (RBF)**
Capital repaid as a percentage of monthly revenue until a fixed repayment cap is reached. Providers include Pipe, Clearco, and Capchase.
- Typical repayment: 6–12% of monthly revenue
- Repayment cap: 1.5–2.5x the amount borrowed
- No covenants or equity dilution
- Requires recurring revenue to underwrite

**Best for:** B2B SaaS with ARR >$500K and stable churn; bridging to a seasonal revenue peak; funding marketing spend with known CAC payback

**Cloud credits**
Free cloud computing credits from AWS, Google Cloud, Azure, and smaller providers via their startup programs.
- AWS Activate: up to $100K for early-stage startups with a lead investor
- Google for Startups: up to $200K for eligible startups
- Azure for Startups: up to $150K
- Credits are often stackable and renewable

**Best for:** every startup should apply; credits for infrastructure reduce burn directly with no strings attached

**Grants and government funding**
Non-dilutive funding from government innovation programs, research grants (SBIR/STTR in the US), and regional startup programs.
- SBIR/STTR: up to $2M for US companies engaged in R&D with government application
- Horizon Europe: significant grants for European startups in research areas
- Regional programs vary widely by country and city

**Best for:** deep tech, health tech, climate tech, or defence-adjacent startups; time-consuming to apply but fully non-dilutive

## When to use and when to avoid

| Scenario | Verdict |
|----------|---------|
| Post-equity round, extending runway by 3–6 months | Consider venture debt |
| Pre-revenue, no equity raised | Avoid; covenants will constrain you |
| $500K+ ARR, stable growth | Consider RBF for marketing or capex |
| Infrastructure costs eating runway | Apply for cloud credits immediately |
| Bridge to close a deal you're already confident in | Consider; but model worst case if the deal falls through |
