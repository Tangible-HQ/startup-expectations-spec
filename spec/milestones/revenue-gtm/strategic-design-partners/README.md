# Strategic design partners

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Customers & revenue

> Bring in one or two reference customers whose name, use case, and co-development input shapes your roadmap and opens doors with future buyers.

---

## What you are aiming for

One or two customers — carefully selected — who are co-developing the product with you, whose name carries weight with your target market, and who are on a clear path to becoming a paid reference. Not five customers with conflicting needs. Not a customer whose use case doesn't generalise. One or two who represent your ICP perfectly and are committed to the partnership.

## What to focus on

- **Select for fit and influence, not just willingness** — the best strategic design partner has the exact use case you're building for and is a recognised name in your ICP's industry; being talked about by them is worth more than a contract
- **Put the commitment in writing** — a strategic design partner agreement should specify: regular meeting cadence, named stakeholders, what they're co-developing, and the path to a paid contract
- **Govern the roadmap** — two design partners with conflicting needs will fracture your roadmap; if they disagree on what to build, you're building for neither; pick partners whose use cases overlap
- **Time-box the relationship** — a design partner who stays free forever is a customer who won't become a reference; agree on a timeline for conversion to paid from the start

## Deprioritize for now

- More than two or three strategic design partners simultaneously — the value is in depth, not breadth
- Design partners in different verticals — they'll pull the roadmap in different directions
- Design partners who want influence but won't commit time — meetings every two weeks, named stakeholders, honest feedback; if they won't do this, they're not a strategic partner

## What makes a design partner "strategic"

A strategic design partner is different from a regular early customer:

| Characteristic | Regular customer | Strategic design partner |
|---------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Name recognition | Not required | High — their name opens doors |
| Use case | Any use case | Exactly the use case you're building for |
| Commitment | Uses the product | Regular calls, feedback, co-development input |
| Roadmap influence | None | Significant — they shape what you build |
| Path to paid | Pays from day one | May start free; converts on a defined timeline |
| Reference value | Helpful | High — actively endorses you to peers |

## The design partner agreement

A simple written agreement (not a full legal contract) should cover:

- **Meeting cadence** — how often you meet and who attends on their side
- **Co-development scope** — the specific features or use cases you're building together
- **Feedback obligations** — what they're expected to provide and by when
- **Pricing path** — the price they'll pay when they convert to a paid customer, and when
- **Reference rights** — whether you can use their name and logo with their permission

## Managing roadmap influence

The biggest risk with design partners is letting them drive the roadmap in a direction that doesn't generalise. Before building anything a design partner requests:

1. Is this problem shared by other customers in the ICP? (talk to 3 others)
2. Would we build this anyway without the partner's influence?
3. Does this feature serve the wedge or distract from it?

If the answer to all three is no, push back — or build it as a one-off with no commitment to maintain it.

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No design partners** | No formal co-development relationships; customers using the product but no structured partnership |
| **L1 — Partner identified** | One or two ideal candidates identified; initial conversations started |
| **L2 — Agreement signed** | Written agreement in place; meeting cadence established; co-development scope defined |
| **L3 — Active co-development** | Regular calls running; partner input shaping roadmap; reference case in progress; conversion to paid on timeline |

## How this connects to other goals

- **First paying users** — design partners complement paying customers; use [first paying users](../first-paying-users-design-partners/README.md) to understand who would pay before selecting design partners whose use case you want to build
- **Feedback loop and metrics** — design partner calls are a structured form of the customer feedback cadence in [feedback loop and metrics](../feedback-loop-metrics/README.md)
- **First case studies** — the best design partners become your first case studies; align on reference rights early so the story is ready when you need it
- **ICP and wedge** — design partner selection should reinforce your ICP, not expand it; use [ICP and wedge](../icp-and-wedge/README.md) as the filter

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** At least one strategic design partner is in a signed agreement, meeting regularly, actively co-developing with you, and on a documented path to becoming a paid customer and reference.
{% endhint %}
