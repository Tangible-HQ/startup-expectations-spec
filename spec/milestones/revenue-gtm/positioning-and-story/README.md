# Positioning and story

**Stage:** Pre-seed · **Category:** Customers & revenue

> Articulate what you do, for whom, and why it matters — in one clear sentence your ICP recognises as true.

---

## What you are aiming for

A one-sentence positioning statement, a website or landing page that reflects it, and a consistent way of describing the company that resonates with target customers — not investors, not the press, not the general public. Positioning that your ICP recognises instantly is positioning that works.

## What to focus on

- **Write for your ICP, not for everyone** — positioning that speaks to everyone speaks to no one; specificity is what makes a prospect feel seen
- **Use the customer's language** — the words your ICP uses to describe their problem are the words your positioning should use; lift phrases directly from discovery interviews
- **Lead with the problem, not the solution** — customers buy because they have a problem; lead with what hurts, then explain how you fix it
- **One-liner first** — get to a single sentence before writing anything else; if you can't say it in one sentence, the positioning isn't clear yet
- **Test it out loud** — say your one-liner to someone who matches your ICP; if they lean in and say "tell me more," it's working; if they look confused, it's not

## Deprioritize for now

- Brand identity, logo design, and visual polish — these are expensive and premature before the message is validated
- SEO and content strategy — optimise for message fit first, then distribution
- Investor-facing narrative — investor positioning and customer positioning are different; do customer positioning first

## The positioning components

**One-liner:** A single sentence that names the ICP, the problem, and how you solve it differently.

> Format: "We help [specific ICP] [do/avoid X] by [unique mechanism] — without [the thing they hate about current alternatives]."
>
> Example: "We help Series A SaaS companies reduce churn by showing them which features drive retention — without requiring a data scientist to interpret the results."

**Category:** What kind of product are you? Not the category you want to create — the category your ICP already understands. Being "the easiest [known thing]" is usually more effective than "a new category of [invented thing]."

**Proof points:** 2–3 specific, concrete claims that support the positioning. Not "trusted by hundreds of companies" — "used by the operations team at Acme Corp to cut weekly reconciliation time from 4 hours to 20 minutes."

**Why now:** Why is this the right moment for this product to exist? A compelling answer makes the story feel inevitable rather than speculative.

## What the website must do

At pre-seed, your website has one job: make a qualified visitor immediately understand what you do and want to talk to you. It does not need to be beautiful. It needs to be clear.

- **Hero headline:** your one-liner, or the problem statement that leads into it
- **Sub-headline:** who it's for and what changes for them
- **Social proof:** one real customer quote or logo if you have it; honest description of the stage if you don't
- **Single CTA:** one action — book a demo, join the waitlist, or start a trial; not three competing options

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No positioning** | No consistent description of what the company does; different founders say different things |
| **L1 — Draft one-liner** | A one-sentence description exists; not yet tested with ICP; website may not reflect it |
| **L2 — Tested and aligned** | One-liner validated in customer conversations; all founders use it consistently; website reflects it |
| **L3 — Resonates with ICP** | Target customers recognise themselves in the positioning; inbound leads reference the problem you named; website converts qualified visitors |

## How this connects to other goals

- **ICP and wedge** — positioning is derived from ICP and wedge; you cannot write effective positioning before [ICP and wedge](../icp-and-wedge/README.md) is confirmed; the wedge is what the positioning leads with
- **Problem–solution validation** — the language customers use in discovery interviews is the raw material for positioning; mine [PSV](../problem-solution-validation/README.md) interviews for exact phrases before writing a word
- **Beta and launch paths** — public launch requires positioning to be in place; don't send traffic to a page that can't convert it
- **First case studies** — strong positioning makes case studies easier to write; the positioning becomes the frame, the customer story becomes the proof

{% hint style="success" %}
**Done when:** You can say your one-liner to a person who matches your ICP and they immediately respond with "yes, that's a real problem" or "how does it work?" — and your website says the same thing.
{% endhint %}
