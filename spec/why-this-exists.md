# Why this project exists

Startups rarely fail because nobody cared. They often struggle because **expectations are implicit**—between co-founders, between founders and early hires, and between the company and the people it serves. That gap shows up early: what “good” means for the **company**, for **each role**, and **at each phase** is fuzzy, and everyone fills in the blanks differently.

This project exists to offer **open, shared alignment**: a plain-language spec anyone can read, fork, and improve so founders and operators can point at the same picture of what matters **now** versus **later**, without pretending every company is identical.

Many founders wrestle with this in the first years—not from lack of ambition, but from lack of a **common vocabulary** for stage, runway, product readiness, revenue motion, team shape, and trust. This repo is one attempt to make that vocabulary **inspectable** and **versionable**, like code.

It is also an **invitation**. If you build tools—checklists, copilots, planning apps, hiring flows, board packs—this spec is meant to be **composed into products** that help startups work in a **product-first** way: grounded in what the company is trying to prove at each milestone, not generic advice. Builders who improve how startups coordinate are welcome to treat this as a foundation layer and ship on top of it.
