# Seed

Get to first repeatable signal — paying users, measurable retention, or a clearly de-risked thesis. The question investors are asking: can you acquire and keep customers without heroics?

**[blocking]** = required before goals listed after it. **[conditional]** = skip if it doesn't apply. Run the customer track and the housekeeping track simultaneously — don't work through this list linearly.

| Phase | Timing | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Housekeeping | First 30 days post-raise | Cap table, burn model, milestone targets — lock these before spending the money |
| Customer traction | Months 1–4 | Paying users and instrumentation — the only evidence that matters for Series A |
| Scaling attempts | Months 3–6 | Reliability, contracts, community — only after you have paying users |
| Series A prep | Months 6+ | Legal, security, and team infrastructure that diligence will check |

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Common traps**

- **Fundraising before customers** — raising seed without paying users means you're selling a story, not traction. You'll raise at a lower valuation with less leverage.
- **Hiring sales before founders can sell** — if you can't close deals yourself, a sales hire won't either. Founders must own the first 5–10 closes.
- **Too many design partners** — four or more with different needs will fracture your roadmap. One or two with aligned use cases is better.
- **Confusing activity with traction** — meetings, signups, and LOIs are not traction. Retention and payment are.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="success" %}
**Ready for Series A when…**

- Named customers paying real money with measurable retention or repeat usage
- At least one customer acquisition path that works without founder involvement
- Team of 5–12 with clear ownership across eng, product, and customer-facing roles
- You can articulate why the next $X million makes the motion faster, not just bigger
{% endhint %}
