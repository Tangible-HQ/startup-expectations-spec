# Series A

Systematize what works. You are no longer finding the answer — you are scaling it. Every decision should reduce founder dependency and increase repeatability. Investors are watching whether you can build a company, not just a product.

**[blocking]** = required within the first 90 days. **[conditional]** = skip if it doesn't apply to your company type. Goals 1–3 are the foundation everything else depends on — run them before anything parallel.

| Phase | Timing | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Systemize | First 90 days | Board reporting, repeatable GTM, first case studies — signal that you're operating, not still experimenting |
| Extend reach | Months 3–9 | New channels, international signals, product expansion — only after core motion is documented |
| Institutional readiness | Months 6+ | Compliance, security maturity, insurance — gating requirements for enterprise and Series B diligence |
| Team scale | Ongoing | Remote hiring, immigration, advisor governance as you grow beyond the founding core |

---

1. [Board and investor reporting](../milestones/fundraising-runway/board-investor-reporting-rhythm/README.md) — establish a regular update rhythm with clear metrics, asks, and decisions — not just good news **[blocking — establish within 30 days of close]**
2. [Early go-to-market motion](../milestones/revenue-gtm/early-gtm-motion/README.md) — document the sales playbook: ICP, discovery, objection handling, and close criteria **[blocking — document before hiring a sales team]**
3. [First case studies and references](../milestones/revenue-gtm/first-case-studies/README.md) — turn your best customers into written proof points and reference calls for new prospects
4. [Partnerships and channels](../milestones/revenue-gtm/partnerships-and-channels/README.md) — identify resellers, integration partners, or distribution ecosystems that multiply your reach
5. [International expansion](../milestones/revenue-gtm/international-expansion/README.md) — open a second market only after your domestic playbook runs without founder involvement **[conditional — only after domestic motion is proven]**
6. [Product surface expansion](../milestones/product-delivery/product-surface-expansion/README.md) — add features or a second product line only when the core is defensible and the team can sustain it
7. [Compliance for your market](../milestones/trust-compliance/compliance-for-your-market/README.md) — SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, or HIPAA depending on what your customers and contracts require
8. [Regulatory and industry rules](../milestones/trust-compliance/regulatory-industry-rules/README.md) — map sector-specific regulations your customers operate under and that touch your product
9. [Ethics and misuse prevention](../milestones/trust-compliance/ethics-and-misuse/README.md) — define acceptable use policies and build controls before your product gets used in ways you didn't intend
10. [Security and privacy posture](../milestones/trust-compliance/security-and-privacy-posture/README.md) — mature from "we care about security" to documented controls, pen tests, and a functioning security program
11. [Incident readiness](../milestones/trust-compliance/incident-readiness/README.md) — upgrade from a basic playbook to a tested process with named roles, runbooks, and post-mortems
12. [Insurance when it matters](../milestones/operations-back-office/insurance-d-and-o-epli/README.md) — D&O, E&O, and cyber liability coverage before your first enterprise contract requires it
13. [Dual mission or structure](../milestones/trust-compliance/dual-mission-structure/README.md) — B Corp certification, a nonprofit arm, or climate reporting **[conditional — B Corp, nonprofit arm, or climate reporting only]**
14. [Deep tech and R&D milestones](../milestones/product-delivery/deep-tech-r-and-d-milestones/README.md) — stage-gate research against commercial timelines so the board can track progress **[conditional — science-heavy companies only]**
15. [Hardware and physical goods](../milestones/product-delivery/hardware-and-physical-goods/README.md) — supply chain, manufacturing partners, and certifications for physical products **[conditional — hardware companies only]**
16. [Pivot or scope cut](../milestones/product-delivery/pivot-or-scope-cut/README.md) — know when to change direction, how to communicate it, and how to preserve morale through the change
17. [Advisors versus operators](../milestones/hiring-team/advisors-versus-operators/README.md) — distinguish people who give advice from people who get things done; don't over-index on the former
18. [Remote and global hiring](../milestones/hiring-team/remote-and-global-hiring/README.md) — build a hiring process and compensation philosophy that works across geographies and time zones
19. [Immigration and work authorization](../milestones/hiring-team/immigration-and-work-authorization/README.md) — understand visa options for key hires and set up a process before it becomes urgent

---

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Common traps**

- **Hiring executives before processes exist** — a VP Sales with no playbook, no CRM, and no defined ICP has nothing to manage. Document the motion first.
- **International before domestic** — opening a London office before your home market motion is repeatable doubles operational complexity with no new signal.
- **Adding product surface before the core is defensible** — a second product line that splits engineering attention often kills the first one.
- **Board reporting as performance** — updates that only show good news train your board to be useless in a crisis. Use reporting to surface real blockers.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="success" %}
**Ready for Series B when…**

- Sales cycle is documented, with a playbook a non-founder can execute
- Gross margin is understood, measured, and trending in the right direction
- Key executive roles filled — at minimum VP/Head of Sales, Engineering, and Product
- You can state the Series B thesis in one sentence: "We're scaling X motion into Y market, which unlocks Z"
{% endhint %}
